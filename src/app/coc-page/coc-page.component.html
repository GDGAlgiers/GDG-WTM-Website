Code of Conduct

GDG Algiers & WTM Algiers are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, speakers, sponsors,organizers and participants. Committed to creating an environment that is safe, professional and of mutual trust where diversity and inclusion are valued, and where everyone is entitled to be treated with courtesy and respect. We do not tolerate harassment in any form. Our organisers commit to making conferences, workshops, hackathons and all associated activities productive and enjoyable for everyone.

This code of conduct applies to all DevFest Algiers spaces, both online and offline. Anyone who violates this code of conduct may be sanctioned or expelled from all our activities. Some spaces may have additional rules in place, which will be made clearly available to everyone.

We expect everyone to follow these rules at all event venues, online interactions in relation to the event, and on event supplied transportation.

Our Commitement

Speakers, Sponsors, Organizers and Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately. If a participant engages in harassing behavior, RESPONSE TEAM may take any action they deem appropriate, up to and including expulsion from all GDG Algiers and WTM Algiers spaces or events and identification of that person as a harasser to other GDG Algiers or WTM Algiers members or the general public. We will be happy to help participants contact venue security or local law enforcement, provide escorts, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the event if needed.

Our beliefs And Rules

Our beliefs stand for inclusivity. We believe that every single person has the right to learn new things, share their knowledge, grow their network and have fun in a safe and welcoming environment.

Booth staff (including volunteers) should not use sexualised clothing/uniforms/costumes, or otherwise create a sexualised environment.

Note that participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately. Sponsors, judges, mentors, volunteers, organizers, and anyone else at the event are also subject to the anti-harassment policy. In particular, attendees should not use sexualised images, activities, or other material during the event.

If participants wish to share photos of a speaker on social media, we strongly recommend that they first get the speaker’s permission. Participants may also share the contents of talks/slides via social media unless speakers have asked that specific details/slides can not be shared.

Be respectful and behave professionally. Harassment and abuse are never tolerated. If you are in a situation that makes you uncomfortable at any of our events, if interacting with one of our representatives or event organizers makes you uncomfortable, please report it using the procedures included in this document.

  • Pattern of inappropriate social contact, such as requesting/assuming inappropriate levels of intimacy with others.
  • Harassing photography or recording, including logging online activity for harassment purposes
  • Incitement of violence towards any individual, including encouraging a person to commit suicide or to engage in self-harm.
  • offensive verbal or written comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, disability, mental illness, neuro(a)typicality, physical appearance, body size, age, race, or religion.

GDG Algiers & WTM Algiers prioritize marginalized people’s safety over privileged people’s comfort. RESPONSE TEAM reserves the right not to act on complaints regarding

  • ‘Reverse’ -isms, including ‘reverse racism,’ ‘reverse sexism,’ and ‘cisphobia’
  • Reasonable communication of boundaries, such as “leave me alone,” “go away,” or “I’m not discussing this with you.”
  • Communicating in a ‘tone’ you don’t find congenial
  • Criticizing racist, sexist, cissexist, or otherwise oppressive behavior or assumptions

Things to keep in mind

At a DevFest Algiers,
you agree to

  • Behave professionally and respectfully with everyone
  • Adopt Diversity and Inclusion
  • Avoid aggressively pushing your own services, products or causes.
  • Respect confidentiality requests by speakers and other attendees
  • Look out for one another

These behaviors are not
tolerated

  • Invasion of privacy, including taking photos (or requesting selfies) of people who refuses it.
  • Being disruptive, stalking, following or threatening anyone.
  • Homophobia, racism or behavior that discriminates against a group or class of people.
  • Sexual harassment of any kind, including unwelcome sexual attention and inappropriate physical contact.
